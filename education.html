Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if an item is recyclable? +

Look for recycling symbols on the item which indicate whether it can be recycled. Most plastics have a number inside a triangle that identifies the type of plastic. Items 1 and 2 are generally accepted everywhere, while others may vary by location.

When in doubt, check your local recycling guidelines or use our recycling search tool to find specific information for your area.

Do I need to clean items before recycling them? +

Yes, items should be relatively clean before recycling. Food residue can contaminate entire batches of recyclables. Rinse containers and remove food scraps, but don't waste excessive water - they don't need to be spotless.

What happens to my recycling after it's collected? +

After collection, recyclables are taken to a Materials Recovery Facility (MRF) where they're sorted by type (plastic, paper, metal, glass). These sorted materials are then compressed into bales and sold to manufacturers who process them into new products.

How can I reduce my household waste? +

There are many strategies to reduce household waste:

  • Buy products with minimal packaging
  • Use reusable shopping bags, water bottles, and food containers
  • Compost food scraps and yard waste
  • Repair items instead of replacing them
  • Buy in bulk to reduce packaging waste
  • Choose durable, long-lasting products
What should I do with hazardous waste? +

Hazardous waste like batteries, electronics, paint, and chemicals should never go in regular trash or recycling. Most communities have special collection events or dedicated drop-off locations for hazardous waste. Check our hazardous waste guide for specific instructions based on material type.
